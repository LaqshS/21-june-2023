Popular Landmarks in Jalandhar

1. Rangla Punjab Haveli

Location: GT Road, Jalandhar

Rangla Punjab Haveli is a cultural village and restaurant that showcases the vibrant Punjabi culture. It features traditional Punjabi architecture, folk music, dance performances, and serves authentic Punjabi cuisine. Visitors can experience the rich heritage of Punjab and enjoy the lively ambiance.

2. Devi Talab Mandir

Location: Tanda Road, Jalandhar

Devi Talab Mandir is a famous Hindu temple dedicated to Goddess Durga. It is known for its sacred pond and the 200-year-old idol of Goddess Durga. The temple attracts a large number of devotees, especially during the Navratri festival. The serene surroundings and religious significance make it a popular landmark in Jalandhar.

3. Wonderland Theme Park

Location: Nakodar Road, Jalandhar

Wonderland Theme Park is a popular amusement park in Jalandhar. It offers a wide range of thrilling rides, water slides, and entertainment options for all age groups. The park also features landscaped gardens, food stalls, and live performances, providing a fun-filled experience for visitors.

4. Shaheed-E-Azam Bhagat Singh Museum

Location: Khatkar Kalan, Jalandhar

Shaheed-E-Azam Bhagat Singh Museum is a historical museum dedicated to the life and contributions of the renowned freedom fighter, Bhagat Singh. It displays various artifacts, photographs, and documents related to his life and the Indian independence movement. The museum serves as a tribute to the brave martyr.

5. St. Mary's Cathedral

Location: Church Road, Jalandhar

St. Mary's Cathedral, also known as Jalandhar Cathedral, is a beautiful Roman Catholic church. It is one of the oldest churches in Punjab and features stunning architecture and intricate artwork. The cathedral is a significant religious landmark and a place of worship for the Catholic community in Jalandhar.
